# Chapter 30 — Why the Powers Would Never Have Crucified Him

<p align="center"><a href="../part-07/chapter-29.md">← Previous</a> | <a href="../part-08/chapter-31.md">Next →</a></p>

---

## A Strange Statement from Paul

In one of the most intriguing statements in the New Testament, Paul writes:

> “Which none of the princes of this world knew: for had they known it, they would not have crucified the Lord of glory.”  
> *(1 Corinthians 2:8)*

This sentence raises an obvious question.

If the crucifixion accomplished the victory of God, why would the rulers of the world have participated in it at all?

Paul’s answer is simple.

They did not understand what they were doing.

If they had understood the outcome, they would never have allowed it to happen.

---

## Who Are the “Rulers of This World”?

Paul’s language invites careful attention.

The “rulers” involved in the crucifixion clearly include human authorities. Roman officials carried out the execution. Jewish leaders demanded it.

Yet Paul’s language often points beyond merely human actors.

Throughout his letters, Paul frequently speaks of unseen powers operating behind earthly systems.

He refers to:

- principalities  
- powers  
- rulers of darkness  
- spiritual forces in heavenly places  

These expressions appear in passages such as Ephesians 6 and Colossians 2.

In Paul’s understanding, the world is not governed solely by visible institutions. It is also shaped by unseen powers that influence human structures and systems.

---

## The Strategy of the Powers

Before the crucifixion, the powers of darkness appear to believe they have succeeded.

Jesus is arrested.  
His followers scatter.  
He is publicly executed.

From every visible perspective, the movement around Jesus collapses.

Yet the New Testament presents the crucifixion very differently.

Instead of destroying Jesus, the cross becomes the moment when the powers themselves are defeated.

Paul writes:

> “Having spoiled principalities and powers, he made a shew of them openly, triumphing over them in it.”  
> *(Colossians 2:15)*

The cross becomes a reversal.

The event the powers believed would end Jesus becomes the event that exposes and defeats them.

---

## Death as the Weapon of the Powers

To understand this reversal, we must recognize the central role of death.

Throughout Scripture, death functions not merely as a biological event but as a ruling power.

Humanity lives under its dominion.

This is why the New Testament repeatedly describes Christ’s work in terms of defeating death itself.

The author of Hebrews writes:

> “That through death he might destroy him that had the power of death, that is, the devil.”  
> *(Hebrews 2:14)*

The powers maintained their dominion through death.

Every human life eventually passed into their domain.

The crucifixion appeared to confirm that pattern.

Another victim had fallen.

But this time the pattern broke.

---

## The Resurrection Changes Everything

The resurrection reveals what the powers did not anticipate.

Jesus enters death but does not remain there.

Death cannot hold Him.

The grave cannot contain Him.

The resurrection exposes death as a defeated master.

From that moment forward, the dominion of death begins to collapse.

Those who unite themselves with Christ share in this victory.

They pass through death with Him and rise into new life.

---

## The Powers Lose Their Claim

If death is the foundation of the powers’ authority, then resurrection removes their claim.

The system that held humanity in bondage begins to unravel.

This explains why the New Testament repeatedly connects salvation with participation in Christ’s death and resurrection.

Believers do not merely admire Christ’s victory.

They enter it.

Through union with Christ:

- the old dominion ends  
- the claim of death is broken  
- new life begins  

What happened to Christ begins to happen to His people.

---

## Why the Powers Were Deceived

Paul’s statement now becomes clearer.

The powers understood death.

They did not understand resurrection.

They knew how to destroy human life.

They did not understand what would happen if a truly righteous life passed through death and emerged victorious.

Their greatest weapon became the instrument of their defeat.

The cross was not merely an execution.

It was a trap.

---

## The Triumph Hidden in Weakness

The irony of the cross runs through the entire New Testament.

What appears weak becomes powerful.

What appears defeated becomes victorious.

What appears shameful becomes glorious.

Paul calls this the wisdom of God—wisdom hidden from the rulers of the age.

The powers could recognize force.

They could not recognize sacrificial love.

They could wield death.

They could not comprehend life that death could not extinguish.

---

## Why This Matters

Understanding the defeat of the powers clarifies the nature of salvation.

Christ did not merely change a legal status.

He broke a dominion.

He did not simply persuade a judge.

He defeated a tyrant.

The gospel therefore announces more than forgiveness.

It announces liberation.

Humanity is being freed from the powers that once ruled through death.

---

## What This Chapter Establishes

The crucifixion was not a tragic accident.

It was the moment when the powers unknowingly participated in their own defeat.

Had they understood the outcome, Paul says, they would never have allowed it.

But they did not understand.

And through that misunderstanding, the dominion of death was broken.

The next chapter will explore how this victory becomes effective in the lives of those who enter into Christ’s death and resurrection.
